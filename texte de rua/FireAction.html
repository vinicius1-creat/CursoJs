Fire action

High-rise blocks are built to withstand fire for sufficient time for the fire brigade to attend and deal with the fire without the need for full evacuation.

Residents in properties not affected by fire or smoke should remain in their home unless advised otherwise by the fire brigade.

If there is a fire in your property:

  • Alert any other people in the property.
  • Leave the room at once and close the door to prevent smoke and fire spreading.
  • Leave the property calmly and close the front door.
  • Leave the building.
  • Don't use balconies unless they lead out.
  • Call the fire brigade on 999.
  • Give the operator your address and flat number.
  • Make yourself known to the fire brigade.

If there is a fire in your building:

  • Stay inside your property if you don't know where the fire is.
  • Close your front door and windows.
  • The fire brigade will guide you if evacuation is requiered
  • Do not use the lift
  • Close your door behind you.
If there is a fire, you should leave the block only if your property is affected by fire or smoke.

Stay in your property if you are not directly affected fire.
